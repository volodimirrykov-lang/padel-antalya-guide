Padel in Antalya — Complete Guide

Antalya has become one of Turkey's main hubs for padel — the racquet sport popular across Spain, Sweden, and Latin America. With year-round mild weather, growing club infrastructure, and a large international community, the city offers dedicated indoor venues and a developing coaching scene.

What padel is

Padel is played on a 20×10 metre enclosed court, with glass walls and a low net. It uses a solid racquet (no strings) and a low-pressure tennis ball. Most games are doubles. The sport originated in Mexico in 1969, was popularised in Spain in the 1970s, and is now among the fastest-growing racquet sports worldwide. It is faster to learn than tennis and is heavily social, which suits Antalya's tourist and community mix.

Where padel is played in Antalya

Antalya has four primary districts with dedicated padel facilities:

  • Konyaaltı — west side of the city. Closest to Konyaaltı beach and tourist accommodation hubs.
  • Muratpaşa — central Antalya. Home to Antalya Padel Club (Yalı Caddesi) and Padel Park Club Lara (Ermenek).
  • Döşemealtı — northern district. Home to Padel Park Arena.
  • Lara / Belek resort zone — several five-star resorts offer padel as part of guest facilities (e.g. Ali Bey, Regnum Carya, Robinson).

When to play in Antalya

Antalya summer temperatures regularly exceed 35°C between June and September. For this reason, dedicated padel clubs in Antalya operate indoor facilities with climate control. This makes year-round play comfortable — including peak summer when outdoor courts elsewhere become uncomfortable.

Off-season (October to April) is mild, with daytime temperatures of 15–24°C, ideal for play and lower court demand.

Languages spoken in Antalya padel clubs

Reflecting Antalya's international mix, club staff typically handle inquiries in Turkish, English, Russian, Ukrainian, and German, though language coverage varies by club and shift.

Tournaments and events

Major Antalya padel clubs run regular tournaments — typically monthly club-level competitions plus seasonal events. Some clubs coordinate their tournament calendars with the Turkish Padel Federation (Türkiye Padel Federasyonu).

For tourists visiting Antalya

If you play padel and are visiting Antalya, see the For Tourists guide. Most clubs offer Open Game sessions — sessions where solo players are paired into mixed-level groups, so you can play without bringing partners.

Equipment

All major clubs rent racquets and sell balls. If you're staying long-term, club pro shops typically carry Spanish and Argentine padel brands (Bullpadel, Adidas Padel, Head Padel). Rental racquets are usually 10–15 EUR per session.

Pricing typical range

Court rental in Antalya is usually 30–60 EUR per hour for the court (4 players split), depending on club tier, time of day, and weekday vs. weekend. Coaching lessons typically run 25–50 EUR per hour per player. Membership programs and prepaid balance options offered by some clubs typically reduce per-game cost.
