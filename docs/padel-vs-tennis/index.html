Padel vs tennis — the difference in plain words

The shortest honest answer: padel is like tennis, but easier to start and more social. The scoring is identical (15/30/40, games, sets), the court is about a third of the size, you always play doubles, the serve is underhand, and the glass walls keep the ball in play — so rallies last longer and your first game is already fun. That is why padel is the fastest-growing racket sport in the world, and why so many tennis players in Antalya now play both.

What is the main difference between padel and tennis?

The court. Padel is played 2 vs 2 on an enclosed 10x20 m court — roughly a third of a tennis court — with glass walls that are part of the game: the ball can bounce off them and stay in play, like in squash. Tennis has no walls and is mostly played without them ever touching the rally. Scoring is the same in both sports.

Is padel easier than tennis?

For a beginner, yes — significantly. The underhand serve removes the hardest tennis skill on day one, the smaller court means less running, the solid perforated racket is easier to control than a strung one, and the walls give you a second chance on balls you would lose in tennis. Most people enjoy their very first padel game; in tennis that usually takes weeks of lessons.

I play tennis. Will I be good at padel?

You will start faster than most — your reading of the ball, footwork and net game transfer directly. Two habits need rewiring: swing shorter (a tennis swing sends the ball off the back glass) and learn to let the ball pass you and play it off the wall instead of chasing every ball before the bounce. Most tennis players feel comfortable within two or three sessions.

Is padel more social than tennis?

That is the other big practical difference. Padel is doubles by design, so it is four people on court, and open-play formats like americano mix partners every few games — you can come alone and leave with new friends. This is a large part of why clubs in Antalya fill padel courts through community events rather than court rental alone.

Do padel and tennis use the same ball?

The balls look identical, but a padel ball has slightly lower pressure, so it bounces a bit less — which matters on a small court with walls. Rackets are completely different: padel uses a solid, perforated racket with no strings, shorter and easier to handle than a tennis racket.
