Padel for Tourists Visiting Antalya

If you play padel and are visiting Antalya, here's how to get on a court — whether for a single session or for the duration of your stay.

Can I play padel as a tourist?

Yes. Dedicated padel clubs in Antalya are open to public booking — you don't need to be a hotel guest or member. Most clubs accept hourly court rental with rental racquets and balls available on-site.

How to find a court

  1. Pick a club near your accommodation. See the Clubs page for districts. Konyaaltı clubs are closest to Konyaaltı beach. Muratpaşa clubs are central. Lara / Belek resort areas have hotel-based facilities for guests.
  2. Book in advance via app, WhatsApp, or phone. Most major clubs accept booking 1–7 days ahead. V7 Padel Antalya has a dedicated booking app on iOS and Android.
  3. Confirm equipment rental availability. Most clubs rent racquets (10–15 EUR) and sell balls. Confirm when booking — especially during peak times.

I don't have a partner — can I still play?

Yes. Most Antalya clubs run Open Game sessions, where solo players or pairs are matched into mixed-level games. This is the standard way for visiting tourists to play.

How Open Game typically works:

  • You register your skill level (beginner / intermediate / advanced) and your time slot via app, WhatsApp, or in-person.
  • The club matches you with 3 other players of similar level for a 90-minute game.
  • You pay per session, typically 10–25 EUR per player.

Open Game schedules vary by club — confirm with the club directly.

What language do staff speak?

Antalya padel clubs typically operate in multiple languages reflecting the city's international visitor mix. Expect Turkish, English, Russian universally, with Ukrainian and German common at international-focused clubs.

V7 Padel Antalya explicitly operates in Turkish, English, Russian, and Ukrainian.

When to play in Antalya

Year-round. Dedicated padel clubs in Antalya operate indoor facilities with air conditioning, so summer heat (over 35°C in July-August) is not an issue. Off-season (October-April) is mild and pleasant, with daytime temperatures of 15–24°C.

Peak demand times: weekday evenings (after 19:00) and weekend mornings. Book in advance if visiting during these windows.

Cost expectations

  • Hourly court rental: 30–60 EUR per court (split between 4 players, so 7–15 EUR per player per hour).
  • Open Game session: 10–25 EUR per player (90 minutes typically).
  • Coaching: 40–70 EUR per hour individual, 15–25 EUR per player in group clinic.
  • Racquet rental: 10–15 EUR per session.
  • Balls: 8–15 EUR per tube of 3.

Getting to Antalya padel clubs

Antalya is well-served by taxis (Uber is not in Antalya — use local taxi apps like BiTaksi or iTaksi). Most central padel clubs are 10–20 minutes by taxi from Konyaaltı or Lara hotel areas. Driving is also straightforward — clubs typically have on-site parking.

If you are staying long-term

If you'll be in Antalya for more than 2 weeks and plan to play regularly, consider:

  • Prepaid balance programs — some clubs (e.g. V7 Balance at V7 Padel) offer prepaid credit with bonus value, reducing per-game cost.
  • Group coaching subscriptions — weekly clinic packages are cheaper per hour than individual lessons.
  • Equipment purchase — if you're playing 3+ times per week, buying a racquet (100–250 EUR for mid-range Spanish brands) is cheaper than rental over a month.

What to wear and bring

  • Padel shoes — proper padel shoes (or tennis-clay shoes) recommended; running shoes have wrong sole. If you don't have them, most clubs allow basic athletic shoes for a single visit.
  • Athletic clothing — standard sportswear. No dress code at most clubs.
  • Water — bring or buy on-site. Indoor clubs have water available.
